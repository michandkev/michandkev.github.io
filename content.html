travel.

GETTING TO CABO

Please fly into Los Cabos International Airport (SJD San José del Cabo International Airport). You will need a valid passport to enter Mexico—make sure your passport is not expired!

Travel delays and long customs lines are increasingly common in Cabo, so we suggest flying in a few days before the wedding.

AIRPORT TRANSPORTATION

We highly recommend arranging transportation from the airport to your accommodations before arriving in Cabo. The taxi drivers at the airport can be pushy, and Ubers cannot enter the airport for pick-ups.

TRAVEL CONCIERGE

Our wedding planning team's travel concierge, Daniela Becerril, is available to assist with making restaurant reservations and booking tours and activities for your trip.

Reach out directly to Daniela at travel.takeloveeverywhere@gmail.com or +52 (624) 241 7667 for any itinerary planning assistance you might need!

faq.

  • Where will the wedding events be held?

    The welcome dinner, wedding ceremony, and reception will be held at Nobu Hotel Los Cabos, about 1 hour from the airport by car.

    Upon arrival to the venue, there will be signage and concierge to help direct you to the right location.

  • What’s the easiest way to get to and from the venue?

    If you’re not staying with us at Nobu, the easiest way to get to the venue is by Uber or taxi. Wait times for Uber can be long, so please plan accordingly. When arriving, please tell the front gate that you’re here for the Hsu-Wong wedding.

    The Nobu concierge will be able to assist with calling taxis after the reception is over.

  • Can I bring a plus one?

    We’ve decided to have an intimate wedding with our closest friends and family. Please check your invitation, as any plus ones will be explicitly listed there.

  • How will dietary restrictions be accommodated?

    When you RSVP online, we will ask for your choice of entrée and if you have any dietary restrictions or food allergies.

  • What should I wear?

    The dress code for our wedding ceremony and reception is formal attire: a suit and tie for gentlemen and a floor-length or midi dress for ladies. Colors to avoid for ladies: all white, beige/neutral champagne, bright red.

    The dress code for our welcome dinner is dressy casual: a shirt with collar for gentlemen and a sundress or jumpsuit for ladies.

    See here for inspiration.

  • Will the wedding be indoors or outdoors?

    The ceremony and cocktail hour will be held outdoors on the terrace, weather permitting. The reception will be indoors.

  • What will the weather be like?

    In April, temperatures in Cabo are generally mild, with highs in the mid-80’s °F (25-30°C) and lows in the mid-60’s °F (15-20°C).

    Since the Nobu Los Cabos property is on the Pacific coastline, it can get windy, especially in the evening. We recommend bringing a shawl or light jacket.

  • Can I take pictures with my phone / camera during the wedding?

    We will be having an unplugged ceremony and request that you turn off all phones and cameras to be fully present with us.

    You are more than welcome to take photos and videos before the ceremony begins and during the cocktail hour / reception!

explore cabo.

BARS & RESTAURANTS

  • La Lupita Taco & Mezcal – one of our favorite spots in Cabo featuring a variety of delicious tacos and local mezcal
  • Tacos Gardenias – a hole in the wall spot for fresh seafood tacos (our favorite was the battered fish taco)
  • Manta – upscale fine dining by Chef Enrique Olvera; also a great spot to grab cocktails and watch the sunset
  • Los Tres Gallos – casual spot for traditional Mexican cuisine
  • Flora Farms – farm-to-table restaurant in a beautiful setting on a working farm; reservations recommended

ACTIVITIES

  • Take a water taxi or boat cruise to see El Arco (The Arch)
  • Go zip-lining and off-roading on an ATV through the desert
  • Day trip to Isla del Espiritu Santo (La Paz) to swim with sea lions; 2 hours north of Cabo San Lucas
  • Day trip to Todos Santos, a quaint, artsy town with beautiful beaches; 1 hour north of Cabo San Lucas
